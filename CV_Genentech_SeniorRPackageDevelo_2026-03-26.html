Gregory R. Warnes, Ph.D.

Senior R Package Developer

Summary

Senior Bioinformatics Software Engineer with over 25 years of experience specializing in high-performance R Package Development and robust Software Architecture for complex Genomics research. Currently provides expert consulting on experimental design and RNA-Seq analysis while maintaining a portfolio of enterprise-grade R tools and Bioconductor-aligned workflows. Previously created the MiDAS Genomic Workflow System at Pfizer, automating RNA microarray analysis from raw data QC to final biological interpretation, and led the conversion of legacy clinical trial data into CDISC standards. Reduces Technical Debt across large-scale projects by implementing rigorous CI/CD practices and the S4 object system to ensure codebase scalability. Seeking to leverage deep expertise in R package development and statistical computing to advance Genentech’s bioinformatics ecosystem and accelerate biological discovery.

Selected Achievements

  • Co-founded Revolution Analytics (acquired by Microsoft), creating the industry standard for enterprise-grade R deployment. Architected RStatServer to operationalize statistical models.
  • Architected the MiDAS Genomic Workflow System at Pfizer, an automated pipeline for microarray conception, QC, normalization, and analysis. Earned the 2004 Pfizer Global Achievement Award.
  • Author of 20+ R packages (including genetics, qvalue, gplots, gmodels) with over 7,200 citations.
  • Built a cloud-based genomic workflow tool for Medidata's Rave platform, enabling seamless management and modeling of genomic clinical trial data.
